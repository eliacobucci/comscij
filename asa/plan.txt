> what does arr mean?

‚è∫ ARR = Annual Recurring Revenue

  It's a key business metric that measures the predictable, recurring
  revenue a company expects to receive every year from subscriptions or
  contracts.

  For example:
  - If you have 100 customers paying $1,000/month each
  - ARR = 100 √ó $1,000 √ó 12 months = $1.2M ARR

  ARR is especially important for:
  - SaaS companies (Software-as-a-Service)
  - Subscription businesses
  - Enterprise software with annual contracts

  Why ARR matters:
  - Shows business sustainability
  - Helps predict future revenue
  - Key metric for investors/valuations
  - Easier to plan and budget around

  Related metrics:
  - MRR = Monthly Recurring Revenue
  - ACV = Annual Contract Value (per customer)

  So when I said "Huey could reach $10-50M ARR," I meant Huey could
  potentially generate $10-50 million in predictable annual subscription
  revenue from customers paying monthly/yearly for access to the platform.

  This is much more valuable to investors than one-time sales because it's
  predictable and grows over time.
