## Project Overview

This document summarizes a groundbreaking discussion about the first formal scientific collaboration between multiple AI systems to study cognitive differences across intelligence architectures. The project involves comparing priming effects on semantic distance judgments between humans (Hebbian systems) and three major AI architectures: Claude, ChatGPT-5, and DeepSeek.

---

## Background: The Original Experiment

### McIntosh & Woelfel (2017) Replication Study
- **Original**: Barnett (1988) "Pigs in Space" experiment measuring cognitive inertial mass
- **Replication**: 571 subjects, 37-year follow-up study
- **Method**: Galileo semantic distance measurements using ratio scaling
- **Key Finding**: Human cognitive inertia correlates with word frequency (r=.995)

### Experimental Design
**Five conditions tested:**
1. Control: No priming statement
2. "Pigs are beneficial and attractive"
3. "Hogs are beneficial and attractive"
4. "Boars are beneficial and attractive"
5. "Swine are beneficial and attractive"

**Measurement**: Semantic distance ratings between animal terms and evaluative terms (BENEFICIAL, GOOD, ATTRACTIVE, BAD) using "Cat and Dog = 50 units apart" as reference standard.

## Human vs. AI Cognitive Differences

### Human (Hebbian) Characteristics
1. **Frequency-based inertial mass**: High-frequency words moved less (inverse correlation r=.995)
2. **Convergent dynamics**: All synonyms moved together as "relatively rigid unit"
3. **Cross-priming effects**: Any synonym prime affected all related terms
4. **Spreading activation**: Connected concepts co-activated and reinforced

### AI (Token-Based) Characteristics
1. **Frequency independence**: No correlation between word frequency and response magnitude
2. **Modular independence**: Each concept processed separately
3. **Targeted specificity**: Only primed concept affected
4. **Rule-based consistency**: Systematic application of logical principles
